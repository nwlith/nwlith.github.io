Another Image of Algorithms,
Thesis defended in 2019, Toulouse.
14 × 20 cm, 158 pages.

“Now, in fact, automatism is a fairly low degree of technical perfection. In order to make a machine automatic, it is necessary to sacrifice many of its functional possibilities and many of its possible uses. (...) The real perfecting of machines, which we can say raises the level of technicality, has nothing to do with an increase in automatism but, on the contrary, relates to the fact that the functioning of the machine conceals a certain margin of indetermination.” — Gilbert Simondon

This thesis questions our relationship with our immediate digital environment. I was looking at in visual programming environments, their history, and whether they can enable us to bricoler* with the digital technologies that surround us.

* "Bricoler" can be translated by “to tinker”. “Bricolage” is “the mind in its untamed state as distinct from the mind cultivated or domesticated for the purpose of yielding a return” according to Claude Lévi-Strauss.
